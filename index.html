Introduction

The Government of India has launched various welfare schemes to empower and support women across different sectors. These initiatives aim to address challenges in health, education, employment, and social welfare, striving to create an inclusive and equitable society.

Health Schemes: Programs like Janani Suraksha Yojana and Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana focus on maternal and child health, providing financial aid and essential health services.

Education Schemes: Initiatives such as Beti Bachao Beti Padhao and Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya promote the education of girls, ensuring increased enrollment and retention in schools.

Employment and Entrepreneurship Schemes: Schemes like Mahila E-Haat and Stand Up India support women entrepreneurs with financial aid, market access, and mentorship, fostering economic independence.

Social Welfare Schemes: Programs like the One Stop Centre Scheme and the Women Helpline Scheme offer vital support and protection to women facing violence and abuse.

This website offers detailed information on these schemes, helping women access the resources and support they need. Explore the sections to learn more about the initiatives and their impact on women's lives in India.

Statistics

The implementation of women welfare schemes in India has led to significant improvements in various areas. Here are some key statistics that highlight the impact of these initiatives:

  • Janani Suraksha Yojana: Over 10 million women have benefitted from the Janani Suraksha Yojana, which has significantly reduced maternal and infant mortality rates across the country.
  • Beti Bachao Beti Padhao: The child sex ratio has improved in over 100 districts due to the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao scheme, with an increase in female enrollment in schools by 12% over the past five years.
  • Stand Up India: More than 100,000 women entrepreneurs have received financial assistance and mentorship through the Stand Up India scheme, leading to the creation of numerous small and medium-sized enterprises.
  • One Stop Centre Scheme: The One Stop Centre Scheme has provided support and services to over 300,000 women facing violence and abuse, offering counseling, legal aid, and temporary shelter.
  • Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana: Over 15 million beneficiaries have received financial assistance under the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, which supports pregnant and lactating women to ensure their health and well-being.

These statistics demonstrate the positive outcomes of women welfare schemes, showcasing their vital role in promoting gender equality and empowering women across India.

FAQs

Find answers to frequently asked questions about women welfare schemes in India.

  • Q: What is the eligibility criteria for Janani Suraksha Yojana?

    A: The Janani Suraksha Yojana is available to pregnant women from low-income families. Women must be 19 years or older and should belong to the Below Poverty Line (BPL) category or be scheduled caste/scheduled tribe.

  • Q: How can I apply for the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao scheme?

    A: The Beti Bachao Beti Padhao scheme does not require direct application by individuals. It is implemented through district-level action plans by the government to improve the child sex ratio and promote girl child education.

  • Q: What financial assistance is provided under the Stand Up India scheme?

    A: The Stand Up India scheme offers bank loans between Rs. 10 lakh and Rs. 1 crore to women entrepreneurs for setting up new enterprises in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors.

  • Q: Where can I find One Stop Centres?

    A: One Stop Centres are established in various districts across India. You can find the nearest centre by contacting the women helpline number 181 or visiting the official website of the Ministry of Women and Child Development.

  • Q: How do I register for the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana?

    A: To register for the Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, eligible women can approach Anganwadi centres or health facilities. Registration can also be done online through the PMMVY CAS portal.
